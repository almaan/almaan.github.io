What I listen to

Below you’ll find a curated and moderately updated list of podcast shows that I enjoy listening to.

  • Name: Radiolab

    • Brief description: A science focused podcast with the most odd and fantastic topics. Each episode leaves you full of new thoughts and questions to ponder about, it’s a joy for the mind.
    • Why?: This is one of my favorite podcasts, and one that I’ve followed for 5+ years.
    • Language: English

  • Name: The Jordan Harbinger Show

    • Brief description: Jordan (the host) sits down with people from all branches of life (athletes, spies, scientists) to have some truly mind-blowing conversations.
    • Why?: The range people Jordan manages to bring on to this podcast is nothing but impressive. Every episode gives you new insights into topics that you sometimes even didn’t know existed or that you cared about. Jordan is also a very skillful interviewer.
    • Language: English

  • Name: Theory and Practice

    • Brief description: Interviews with some of the brightest minds in the AI field. Hosted by Google AI and GV.
    • Why?: Here you have the opportunity to listen to their thoughts on topics in machine learning and AI, but also in larger more widely spanning questions.
    • Language: English

  • Name: Tyngre Träningssnack

    • Brief description: Two guys speaking about different aspects of training._
    • Why?: This podcast is educative, has interesting guests, and also operates with an investigative mindset. They present interesting research and studies, but also calls out on bullshit science. Also, importantly, the two guys have a good vibe.
    • Language: Swedish

  • Name: Prestera Mera med Umara

    • Brief description: Two biomedics who breaks down scientific papers and news relating to exercise and phycial performance.
    • Why?: Great guests, interesting and useful content, discussion of relevant questions.
    • Language: Swedish

  • Name: Theory and Practice

    • Brief description: Interviews with some of the brightest minds in the AI field.
    • Why?: Here you have the opportunity to listen to their thoughts on topics in machine learning and AI, but also in larger more widely spanning questions.
    • Language: English

  • Name: People I (Mostly) Admire

    • Brief description: Freakonomics co-author Steven Levitt somehow manages to get an array of high-achieving individuals and asks questions that you didn’t even realize you had. These conversations are always nuanced and quite eye-opening.
    • Why?: Inspiring, amusing, educative, and eloquently put-together interviews.
    • Language: English

  • Name: Presidental

    • Brief description: Produced by the Washington Post and hosted by Lillian Cunningham, this podcast paint illustrative portraits of each of the presidents in American history and current times. These are not sleep-inducing history lessons, but fun and intelligent descriptions of the individuals ending up at the position of POTUS.
    • Why?: It’s both fun and educative, and as someone who’s not taught the names and defining characteristics of each president in school but with an interest in politics and history, these episodes are excellent!
    • Language: English

  • Name: Code Swtich

    • Brief description: A podcast hosted by some excellent people and produced by NPR that “explore how race affects every part of society”. They bring stories about culture, bias, food, history, and technology and how race often has a sublime but important presence in all of these topics.
    • Why?: Coming from Europe where racism and discrimination is present, but not at all to the same extent as in the US this podcast has really helped me to understand some of the challenges that POC and minorities are facing.
    • Language: English

  • Name: Throughline

    • Brief description: Produced by NPR this podcast analyze current events through the lense of history.

    • Why?: Again, as someone with a non-American background, this has helped me immensely to understand some of the current events and why people or groups react the way they do.

    • Language: English

    • Name: Planet Money

    • Brief description: The larger theme is “economy”, put presented with a different narrative. Also, this is not the kind of “boring” economy, but rather how, for example, a computer game (RuneScape) predicted the economic collapse of Venezuela and other quirky phenomena.

    • Why?: Interesting, sparks my curiosity, great stories.

    • Language: English

  • Name: The joy of x

    • Brief description: A podcast hosted by mathematician and author Steven Strogatz (big in chaos theory), who sits down and interview people in the STEM fields.
    • Why?: Great guests, exposure to new and interesting topics, educative. It’s also amazing to hear about the many different paths to success in these fields.
    • Language: English

  • Name: American Scandal

    • Brief description: Documentaries told in more of a “drama” style outlining some of the biggest scandals in American history. Examples are: the Watergate scandal, the DuPont chemical cover up, and the Waco raid.
    • Why?: The way these stories are told are both engaging and informative, the producers have done some really excellent work putting these episodes together and the narrator is also stellar. I also, and this is my personal opinion, find the stories being told in a very unbiased way, outlining the facts but not taking an obvious side.
    • Language: English

  • Name: American Innovations

    • Brief description: Presenting the stories about some of the biggest innovations in American history through a storytelling narrative.
    • Why?: The way these stories are told are both engaging and informative, the producers have done some really excellent work putting these episodes together and the narrator is also stellar. I also, and this is my personal opinion, find the stories being told in a very unbiased way, outlining the facts but not taking an obvious side.
    • Language: English

  • Name: DeepMind: the Podcast

    • Brief description: An inside view of the research that Google AI conducts. This is very non-technical and “pop-sciency”, but still very interesting. This is hosted by Hannah Fry, who’s full of enthusiasm and does an excellent job prodding the researchers about the projects they work on.
    • Why?: Interesting, tech-focused, fun, and educative.
    • Language:

  • Name: 99% Invisible

    • Brief description: Hosted by Roman Mars (who might have the best radio voice in history). This podcast discuss how design influences every aspect of our lives, in ways we never could have expected.
    • Why?: These stories are just so fascinating and amusing, it’s very similar to the “Freakonomics-vibe” in the sense that you often realize how two things that seem completely unrelated actually can have major impacts on each other.
    • Language: English

  • Name: Ultrarunning News

    • Brief description: News in ultrarunning, covering the most high-profile and recent events. Interviews with top athletes and people in the organizations.
    • Why?: If you’re a running nerd like me, with a palate for ultrarunning this is an excellent podcast
    • Language: English
